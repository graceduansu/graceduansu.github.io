About Me

I am currently an undergraduate student at Columbia University majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Applied Mathematics. I am expected to graduate in May 2023. My research interests lie in computer vision, including scene understanding, activity recognition, and 3D perception.

Since Jun. 2020, I have been working on the University of Chicago's DeepScribe computer vision project for automatically transcribing photos of cuneiform tablets with Prof. Sanjay Krishnan. In Jan. 2021, I joined the Columbia University Digital Video and Multimedia Lab and worked on grounded video event extraction under Prof. Shih-Fu Chang. In Jun. 2021, I researched "Concept Whitening for Transfer of Concepts in Imitation Learning" at the Carnegie Mellon University Robotics Institute Summer Scholars Program (CMU RISS) under Prof. Katia Sycara. In Jun. 2022, I researched "Mixed Reality Synthetic Data Generation" at the CMU RISS program under Prof. Srinivasa Narasimhan.

I am also passionate about leading and organizing outreach programs for underrepresented groups in technology.
