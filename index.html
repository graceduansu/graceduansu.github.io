About Me

I'm currently an undergraduate senior at Columbia University in the School of Engineering and Applied Science majoring in Computer Science (Intelligent Systems track) and minoring in Applied Mathematics. I'm expected to graduate in May 2023.

My main research interest is in computer vision. My objective is to research and develop robust computer vision systems that can work in the real world to assist humans by intelligently interpreting their surroundings and learning meaningful, informative visual representations.

To this end, I’ve explored different research areas and built a broad foundation of knowledge in intelligent perception. Since Jun. 2020, I have been working on the University of Chicago's DeepScribe computer vision project for automatically transcribing photos of cuneiform tablets under Prof. Sanjay Krishnan. In Jan. 2021, I joined the Columbia University Digital Video and Multimedia Lab and worked on grounded video event extraction under Prof. Shih-Fu Chang. In Jun. 2021, I researched "Concept Whitening for Transfer of Concepts in Imitation Learning" at the Carnegie Mellon University Robotics Institute Summer Scholars Program (CMU RISS) under Prof. Katia Sycara. In Jun. 2022, I researched "Mixed Reality Synthetic Data Generation" at the CMU RISS program under Prof. Srinivasa Narasimhan.

I'm also passionate about leading and organizing outreach programs for underrepresented groups in technology.
